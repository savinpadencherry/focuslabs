Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 3, 2026

Welcome to FocusChain Labs ("we," "our," or "us"). We are committed to protecting your personal information and your right to privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website (www.focuschainlabs.com) and engage with our services, including AI Product Engineering, Cloud Architecture, Workflow Automation, and Predictive Analytics.

1. Information We Collect

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you express an interest in obtaining information about us or our products and services. The personal information that we collect depends on the context of your interactions with us and the website.

A. Information You Provide to Us

  • Contact Information: Phone numbers, email addresses, and any details you provide via our contact forms, such as scoping calls or queries.
  • Business Information: Details about your company, workflows, and the specific business problems you are looking to solve using our platforms.

B. Information Collected Automatically

  • Usage Data: We automatically collect certain information when you visit, use, or navigate the site, including our Live Build Lab and Command Center simulators. This includes device and usage information, such as your IP address, browser and device characteristics, operating system, language preferences, referring URLs, and information about how and when you use our site.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect or receive for the following business purposes:

  • To provide and manage our services: Designing, building, and deploying cloud applications, AI/ML workflows, and dashboards tailored to your requirements.
  • To communicate with you: Responding to your inquiries, scheduling scoping calls, and providing implementation and launch support.
  • To improve our platform: Analyzing how users interact with our Live Build Lab and website to optimize user experience and service delivery.
  • For marketing and lead funnel automation: Sending administrative information, service updates, and relevant marketing communications. You can opt out at any time.

3. Data Processing for AI and Cloud Services

As a provider of AI/ML workflow automation and predictive analytics, such as credit analyzers and churn signals, we often process data on behalf of our clients. In these instances, FocusChain Labs acts as a Data Processor, and our clients act as the Data Controllers.

  • We do not use your proprietary business data or your customers' data to train public AI models without your explicit consent.
  • All client data processed through our secure cloud backends and IoT-connected mobile experiences is handled in strict compliance with the architecture and security guardrails agreed upon during the scoping and blueprint phase.

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We only share information with your consent, to comply with laws, to provide you with services, to protect your rights, or to fulfill business obligations. We may share your data with:

  • Cloud Service Providers: Third-party vendors who host our servers and cloud infrastructure, such as AWS, GCP, or Azure.
  • Analytics Providers: Tools that help us measure website performance and consumer insights.
  • Legal Requirements: When reasonably necessary to comply with applicable law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request.

5. Data Security

We prioritize the security of the systems we build and the data we hold. We have implemented appropriate technical and organizational security measures designed to protect the security of any personal information we process. This includes secure cloud architectures, authentication, Role-Based Access Control (RBAC), and multi-region databases. However, despite our safeguards, no internet transmission is 100% secure, so we cannot promise or guarantee that hackers, cybercriminals, or other unauthorized third parties will not be able to defeat our security.

6. Data Retention

We will only keep your personal information for as long as it is necessary for the purposes set out in this privacy notice, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law, such as tax, accounting, or other legal requirements.

7. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your geographic location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • The right to access the personal information we hold about you.
  • The right to request that we correct any inaccurate information.
  • The right to request the deletion of your personal information.
  • The right to opt out of marketing communications.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details provided below.

8. Updates to This Policy

We may update this privacy notice from time to time to reflect changes to our practices, operational requirements, or legal regulations. The updated version will be indicated by an updated "Last Updated" date at the top of this document.
